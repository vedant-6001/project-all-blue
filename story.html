My Story // The Builder's Log

In the world of One Piece, the All Blue is a mythical sea where waters from across the globe unite, representing a perfectly balanced and thriving ocean. Growing up in a bustling urban environment, the deep sea isn't something I see daily, yet it dictates the rhythm of my life. The ocean sustains our global climate by driving seasonal weather patterns and regulating the very air we breathe. It also silently protects us, acting as a massive global sink for carbon and heat. Because this protection is largely invisible, humanity often takes it for granted, like an unread Poneglyph holding crucial history we fail to decipher.

As a Grade 11 CBSE student passionate about computer science and ocean engineering, my connection to the ocean is through the lens of a shipwright and problem solver. Inspired by the incredible underwater adventures and technological marvels in 'The Deep' animated series, I want to use robotics to actively explore and protect marine ecosystems. Designing a submersible mini ROV for my university portfolio serves as my ultimate engineering challenge, requiring me to navigate complex physical constraints like waterproofing, buoyancy, and hydrostatic pressure. By combining advanced coding, structural design, and physical hardware, I want to give the ocean a digital voice and build tools that empower conservation. Protecting our waters requires decisive action. We can build our own All Blue.
